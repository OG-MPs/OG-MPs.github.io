OGMP: Oracle Guided Multimodal Policies for Agile and Versatile Robot Control

Lokesh Krishna              Nikhil Sobanbabu              Quan Nguyen

Paper to be submitted

Abstract

Synthesizing agile and versatile control for highly dynamic and inherently unstable robots to solve a given task satisfactorily is an open challenge. When given computationally tractable models, model-based optimal control and model-free learning, when provided with heuristics or task-specific hacks, have shown compelling empirical results. However, a more systematic approach that exploits the available structure and hierarchy in the problem while being flexible enough to benefit from any known priors like first principles is absent. To this end, in this work, we introduce an approach that utilizes any task-based priors through oracles and leverages task-vital multimodality with multimodal policies. Such oracles being sub-optimal in general, we propose to use them to guide the multimodal policy's exploration in training while providing latent mode plans during inference. Our method, being non-restrictive compared to a typical planner and tracking-controller architecture, results in a single multimodal policy that learns a rich collection of behaviors with diverse variations of interpretable underlying parameters. Specifically, we apply our framework to the bipedal parkour task and demonstrate extremely dynamic maneuvers with smooth transitions between the learned behavior variants.
